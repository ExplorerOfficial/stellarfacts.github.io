How has space impacted your life?

We often take space exploration for granted and assume it has no value when compared to other things on this planet. The majority of people typically are not exposed to astronomy very much, so they have no idea how much it actually affects their daily lives. You may not know either, here are some inventions used for space exploration that we use every day.

1950's - Firefighting Equipment

To survive the harsh conditions of space, astronauts needed fire resistant clothing and space suits as well a cooling system to keep them warm in the cold temperatures of space. This helped develop fire resistant clothing for firefighters and cooling system which prevent firefighters from collapsing into the fire due to the heat. The same with breathing systems that prevent firefighters from breathing unhealthy air.

Early 1960's - Bluetooth

NASA partnered with a company called Pacific Plantronics to create the Plantronics Model MS50 Headset to create one of the first lightweight wireless headsets. This was used during the Apollo and Atlas Programs, so astronauts could better communicate with mission control. The use of wireless headsets set the path towards the development of bluetooth devices, one of the most widely used inventions.

1960's - Shock Absorbers

Shock Absorbers were used to protect the space shuttle during launch. Today, they are used to protect buildings from Earthquakes.

1960's - Water Filtering Systems

Water filtering systems were used during NASA's space missions to allow the astronauts to drink clean water. Their methods of water filtering has been standardized today.

1960's - Highway Grooves

As a space shuttle returns, it had to land going very high speeds which means that a wet runway would increase the risk of the shuttle going out of control. The solution was grooves which redirected water away from the runway. Today, this same kind of technology is used to alert drivers that hey are leaving their lane.

1971 - CAT Scans

To recreate lunar pictures during the Apollo Program, NASA developed technology that are used for CAT and MRI scans today. They allow us to look inside the body without the need for surgery.

1979 - Dustbuster

NASA collaborated with Black & Decker to create a cordless handheld vacuum used to extract lunar samples during the Apollo Program which helped develop the handheld vacuums used in some households today.

1981 - Artificial Arms

During the Space Shuttle Program, NASA frequently used robotic arms that could perform tasks such as repairs or inspections without the need for an astronaut present. This technology has helped up build prosthetic limbs which today are used to help motor functions and improve the quality of life for people who have lost a limb.

1983 - Scratch Resistant Lenses

NASA made scratch resistant helmets for astronauts in order to endure the harshness of space to protect the astronauts. Foster Grant, a producer of sunglasses, used this technology in 1983 to make glasses that were scratch resistant.

1986 - Invisible Braces

The need for heat seeking missile systems called for the development of a transparent material called Translucent Polycrystalline Alumina (TPA). This material was great for protecting antennas that were used to track missiles. Today, this material is used for clear orthodontic braces.

1986 - Insulin Pump

NASA needed technology that would keep track of the astronaut's vitals during space missions. So they created the first insulin pumps that were used to monitor blood sugar and pump insulin. This was a breakthrough in the medical industry and is used today to treat people who have diabetes. This has made it easier for those people to treat diabetes and live a better quality life.

1993 - Cameras

NASA developed a type of camera to take detailed pictures of space and other celestial objects. This has contributed to the cameras that you see in your cell phone and computer.

Conclusion

Space exploration isn't just something we do for fun, it's significantly helped develop the technologies that are used every single day. Some people may think NASA should be defunded, but those same people are using every day items that were made possible because we needed alternatives to work in space. NASA has shaped the way millions of people live, hopefully now you can see the importance of NASA.
